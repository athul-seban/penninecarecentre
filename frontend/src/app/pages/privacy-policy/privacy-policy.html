Information Governance & Compliance

At Pennine Care Centre, we are dedicated to protecting the privacy, dignity, and personal data of our residents, their families, and our staff. This policy describes how we collect, store, and process personal and sensitive information in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the UK Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Information We Collect

To deliver exceptional care and safety, we collect and process certain personal information, which may include:

  • Personal Details: Names, contact details, dates of birth, and next-of-kin information.
  • Health & Medical Records: Medical histories, clinical assessments, dietary needs, care plans, and medication administration records.
  • Financial Information: Billing addresses, bank details, and funding support data for invoicing purposes.
  • Digital Data: Cookies, IP addresses, and submission details collected when using our website forms.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal data strictly to fulfill legal, clinical, and administrative requirements, including:

  1. Creating, updating, and delivering custom person-centred care plans.
  2. Ensuring emergency medical practitioners have precise and quick clinical references.
  3. Managing billing, payroll for our employees, and operational compliance.
  4. Responding directly to inquiries submitted through our website and application forms.

3. Data Retention & Protection

All digital data is encrypted using advanced Secure Socket Layer (SSL) protocols. Physical records are kept in secure, restricted-access cabinets. We retain information only for as long as required by law, Care Quality Commission (CQC) standards, or to maintain care continuity.

4. Your Rights

Under UK data protection laws, you retain extensive rights regarding your data. You may request to:

  • Access the personal records we hold about you.
  • Rectify any mistakes or update outdated information.
  • Object to or restrict the processing of non-essential data.
  • Request deletion of data, subject to nursing and medical retention statutory regulations.
